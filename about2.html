About Me

I work for Pitt IT - Client Services at the University of Pittsburgh. Through my work I have developd an acute sense for problem solving and communication. On the job I am presented with problems. I brake down every into componet parts. Then I analyze the parts and leverage a network of experts to solve each smaller problem. This is the process I have become accustomed to whilst building my own cache of knowledge and becoming an expert in my own right.

One area that I have become a leader in is our Jamf support. Jamf is a management framework for Apple computers and devices. I meet regularly with a dedicated team to improve our services. We also hold weekly educational meetings so that the whole team can advance their knowledge. Therefore we are a stronger, more resilient team.

Besides work I have many hobbies including biking, playing synthesizers, and traveling. Biking because I value excercise. Synthesizers for creative expression. Traveling to get fresh perspectives.

I have always enjoyed biking, and recently I have increased my participation in the community by volunteering at bike coop called Free Ride. I share a lot of values with Free Ride including community, diy repair, and volunteering. It exists in an anti-consumerist framework. I enjoy biking to work, and also on Pennsylvania's Great Allegheny Passage trail. My goal is to bike all 300 miles of it this coming year.

With a seamless connection, my technical skills and creative interests harmoniously converge on synthesizers.

I started traveling more as an adult. Through my experiences I have gained friends all over the world. I hope to have a job one day that allows me the freedom to visit them as often as i would like!
